## Process Documentation

### Step 1: Material Science Introduction
We learned about key material properties:
- **Mechanical Properties**: Strength, stiffness, toughness
- **Thermal Properties**: Melting point, thermal expansion
- **Chemical Properties**: Resistance, compatibility
- **Physical Properties**: Density, surface finish

### Step 2: Material Comparison Exercise
[Describe the material comparison exercise]

**Materials Studied:**
1. **PLA (Polylactic Acid)**
   - Properties: Biodegradable, easy to print, moderate strength
   - Best for: Prototypes, educational projects
   - Limitations: Lower temperature resistance

2. **ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)**
   - Properties: Durable, impact-resistant, higher temperature tolerance
   - Best for: Functional parts, enclosures
   - Limitations: Requires heated bed, emits fumes

3. **Wood/Composite Materials**
   - Properties: Natural appearance, varied textures
   - Best for: Aesthetic projects, decorative items
   - Limitations: Less structural strength

**Selection Process:**
1. Identified project requirements
2. Evaluated material properties against requirements
3. Considered fabrication method compatibility
4. Assessed cost and availability

### Step 3: Design Adaptation
[Describe how you adapted a design for different materials]

**Process:**
1. **Original Design Analysis**: Reviewed design for material-agnostic features
2. **Material-Specific Modifications**: 
   - Adjusted wall thicknesses for material strength
   - Modified tolerances for material shrinkage
   - Added support structures where needed
3. **Validation**: Ensured design still meets functional requirements

**Challenges Encountered:**
- **Material Constraints**: Some designs didn't translate well to certain materials
  - *Solution*: Redesigned critical features with material properties in mind

- **Cost vs. Performance**: Balancing material cost with project requirements
  - *Solution*: Created material selection matrix to compare options

## Key Learnings

1. **Material Selection is Critical**: The right material choice can make or break a project.

2. **Design Flexibility**: Understanding materials allows for better design decisions.

3. **Trade-offs**: Every material has strengths and weaknesses—selection involves balancing priorities.
