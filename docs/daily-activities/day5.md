## Process Documentation

### Step 1: Material Preparation
We learned proper material preparation techniques:

**For 3D Printing:**
1. **Filament Handling**
   - Proper storage to prevent moisture absorption
   - Checking for tangles or defects
   - Loading filament correctly into printer

2. **Bed Preparation**
   - Cleaning the build surface
   - Applying adhesion aids (if needed)
   - Leveling the print bed

**For Laser Cutting:**
1. **Material Inspection**
   - Checking for defects or warping
   - Verifying material thickness
   - Ensuring material is flat

2. **Machine Setup**
   - Focusing the laser
   - Setting power and speed parameters
   - Testing on scrap material

### Step 2: Machine Calibration
[Describe the calibration process you performed]

**Calibration Steps:**
1. **Bed Leveling** (for 3D printer)
   - Checked all four corners
   - Adjusted until consistent gap
   - Verified center point

2. **Extruder Calibration**
   - Measured actual vs. expected extrusion
   - Adjusted steps per millimeter
   - Tested with calibration cube

3. **Temperature Tuning**
   - Tested different temperatures
   - Found optimal settings for material
   - Documented settings for future use

### Step 3: First Fabrication
[Describe your first fabrication attempt]

**Process:**
1. **File Preparation**
   - Exported model in correct format
   - Generated G-code (for 3D printing) or cutting paths
   - Reviewed toolpaths for errors

2. **Fabrication Execution**
   - Loaded file into machine
   - Started fabrication process
   - Monitored initial layers/passes

3. **Quality Check**
   - Inspected first results
   - Identified issues
   - Made adjustments

**Challenges Encountered:**
- **Bed Adhesion Issues**: First layer not sticking properly
  - *Solution*: Adjusted bed temperature and first layer height
  
- **Calibration Accuracy**: Initial calibration wasn't precise enough
  - *Solution*: Repeated calibration with finer adjustments

**Solutions Applied:**
- Created calibration checklist
- Documented optimal settings
- Tested with small samples before full prints

## Key Learnings

1. **Preparation is Key**: Proper setup and calibration prevent most fabrication problems.

2. **Documentation Matters**: Recording settings and procedures saves time on future projects.

3. **Patience Required**: Calibration and setup take time but are essential for quality results.
